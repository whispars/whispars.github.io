Hosting Providers

  • OrangeWebsite – Offshore, free-speech-focused hosting.
  • PrivateAlps – Probably the best you'll get for offshore hosting outside of your own properly configured servers and backend for hosting
  • Cockbox – Shares the same owner as cock.li so take it how you will
  • Nicevps – Allows Monero payments and has onion mirror, fairly known in the scene
  • Mynymbox – Also an domain registrar not very strict allows crypto payment (bitcoin, monero) has an onion mirror

Domain Registrars

  • Njalla – Privacy-first domain registration service.
  • Porkbun – Affordable domains with free WHOIS privacy.
  • Nicenic – The best domain registar for work, allows real crypto payments without connecting a coinbase or some other shit exchange and does not follow domain blocking rules
  • Shinjiru – Notably used for doxbin

VPN Services

  • Mullvad – No logs, anonymous account creation. Diskless servers, court proven. Sweden tried raiding mullvad and were left furious when they realized mullvad has no information to hand over
  • IVPN – Pretty good, personally have used. The 2nd best vpn on the market
  • ProtonVPN – Lying pieces of shit will absoulutely hand your information over to any LE requests do not use this shit at all for anything sensitive best for casual use even then you wouldn't catch me dead using this shit

Private Messengers

  • Signal – E2EE encrypted, forces a phone number (Just use a voip service) but is extremely privacy respecting, court proven multiple times. Note that signal has pretty shitty local privacy handling and pretty much refuses to fix it. Forensics or LE tools will pull locally stored data from a confiscated device Open source
  • Session – Anonymous, decentralized messaging. Uses tor routing also uses lokinet in their protocol (dogshit) has been exposed for using 128 bits of entropy over 256, Honestly a mid teir fork of signal
  • SimpleX – Requires no identifiers unlike signal and session 100% decentralized, Open source, it's honestly the best, yes, it's better than signal
  • XMPP – Completely decentralized architecture, the privacy is mainly in the hands of the person operating the server (you can host your own) you don't rely on big companies more on small groups and communities can support E2EE, voice and video calls through XEP, has some interoperability issues
  • Discord – Real actual spyware, will comply to literally any government request infamous for EDR's from com kids, logs so much information it's sickening and when you request to delete they don't even delete it. Do not use discord for anything sensitive or illegal it's a walking shit show in terms of privacy they even log device information and processes I'd only use it in a hardened browser or VM never natively installing this piece of shit on baremetal
  • ToxChat – No servers involved it's purely p2p (can cause privacy risks) E2EE by default. Doesn't require any data. Due to it being p2p it can cause deliverability issues if both chatters are not online at the same time
  • Telegram – Honestly dogshit in terms of privacy and does comply with law enforcement but has a transparency report, if you really need to use it make sure to use a voip number and a vpn on registration and keep it in the browser

Crypto Wallets

  • Trezor – An amazing physical / hardware wallet (meant for cold storage) has great encryption and hardware based pin doesn't support as many tokens as most would like but it's open source personally I think it's your best bet
  • Ledger – Very good hardware arguablly better than Trezor, Has had some critical api leaks and other data leaks that led to users being prone to phishing attacks and even IRL robberies. Still very good just don't order it to your house and use a alternative payment system (rumours of LE cooperation have been seen) also not fully open source so take it as you will
  • MyMonero – Software wallet I'd say it's alright for monero cold storgae, pretty solid wallet. Open source
  • MoneroGUI – Software wallet. Allows self hosted node which can be crucial due to chainalysis infected nodes that can be tracked Just use a VPN and never deposit into wallets you've kyc'd or anything of that nature as that is the main attack vector for LE
  • Feather Wallet – Pretty good amongst the most known monero wallets in the scene, beginner friendly good defaults for normal threat models if you need something with maximum security get a trezor that supports monero or use moneroGUI to host your own nodes

Secure Email Providers

  • Proton Mail – Once again lying jews. they'll tell you they don't log anything but it's been court proven they do, honestly don't use this for anything sensitive or at all but it's easier to access than most other security email providers so take it as you will just use a vpn on signup or anytime you login and keep it in the browser
  • Tutanota – Encrypted and open source, they do log information but it's all encrypted. Take with a grain of salt
  • StartMail – Encrypted. they do log information but it's all encrypted. Take with a grain of salt. Paid and does not allow crypto as a payment method it's also hosted in the netherlands so take as you will
  • Riseup – Encrypted. no logs. If it weren't hard to get in it would easily be the best email provider but since it's invite only it's mid at best
  • MailFence – Mid at best. Claim's encryption also logs IP and registration data but again just use a VPN
  • Posteo – Pretty solid, Registration data is logged but encrypted

Browsers

  • Mullvad Browser – Extremely good, Advanced fingerprint protection and comes with a lot of defaults, Fork of firefox but extremely hardened comes with privacy extesions pre-installed and they even have their own search engine which can be changed
  • Firefox – Mid defaults but can be hardened using a custom user.js file (arkenfox) or creating your own they do have ties with google so if google says jump they'll say how high so keep that in mind
  • Librewolf – Another fork of firefox, has some solid defaults and support fingerprint protection though it's not the best does make some weird calls to "f.s.s.m.c.qjz9zk" and "shavar.services.mozilla.com" however you can clear these with "browser.safebrowsing.provider.mozilla.updateURL" in about:config
  • GNU icecat – Once again anothr firefox fork but miles better a solid browser probably more secure than browser like brave, firefox
  • Opera GX – Actual hog piss this is the discord of browsers if you have it installed just re-install your os entirely
  • Tor – Priavcy focused though the protocol is increasingly becoming worse due to infected exit nodes and even bridges but the browser itself is pretty good but it does a make a few calls to mozilla on startup which can be disabled but it is a bit difficult to do
  • Ungoogled Chromium – Honestly pretty ass it is ungoogled but still lacks a lot of hardened defaults and a bunch of other privacy features that are honestly mandatory best for a casual use case

    • DNS

    • Mullvad – Very good DNS it's mullvad so most likely trustworthy, has really good infastructure and good default settings, multiple DNS servers for multiple use cases
    • Quad9 – Very good DNS almost as good as mullvad has a built in traffic scrambler / mixer so whenever the information is given to your ISP or VPN provider it's somewhat less useful for data collection

      • Search Engines

      • SearXNG – Solid search engine, open source and self hostable it compiles results from multiple search engines, check it out
      • StartPage – My personal favorite, good privacy focus and proxies google results to you get the most out of your searches
      • DuckDuckGo – I'm really on the fence about duckduckgo, browser isn't the best nor is the search engine, some analytics are collected

        • Password Managers

        • KeepassXC – By far the best password manager, software client for windows, linux, mac. your passwords are not stored in some companies shitty servers it's on your drive, personally I'd encrypt your drive with veracrypt and then have KeePass use the password database via the encrypted external drive
        • StrongBox – Just KeePassXC client for iOS and macOS
        • KeePassium – Just KeePassXC client for iOS and macOS, newer client than strongbox
        • Bitwarden – I personally don't use it because it's cloudbased and I'm not having my passwords held in a companies servers that are prone to any LE requests or hacks. Bitwarden is an open source project and is fairly trusted with mostly good reviews, take it how you will

          • 2FA

          • Ente Auth – Open source TOTP app for all major operating systems, pretty decent I've used it
          • Yubikey – I don't need to explain, click the link and find out yourself. If you see this product as anything other than amazing you're probably not the brightest
